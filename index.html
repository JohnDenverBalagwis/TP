Kobe Bryant

One of the best basketball players in the history of the NBA

Here's a time line of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • April 3, 1996 - Kobe anounces he will enter the draft.
  • June 8, 1996 - Kobe Bryant was the 13th overall pick in the 1996 draft. He was dafted by the Charlotte Hornets, who then traded him to the Lakers for Vlade Divac on a Draft Night trade.
  • January 5, 1997 - Kobe Bryant won the NBA Slam Dunk contest during All-Star weekend as a rookie.
  • January 6, 1998 - Kobe was voted as an All-Star and participated in the first All-Star game of his NBA career.
  • June 19, 2000 - Kobe Bryant and the Los Angeles Lakers win the NBA Finals against the Indiana Pacers. This is the first of his five championships.
  • June 15, 2001 - At the age of 22, Kobe helps lead his team to back to back NBA Championships against the Philadelphia 76ers.
  • June 12, 2002 - Kobe and the Lakers complete the 3-peat and win their third consecutive NBA Championship and the third of his career defeating the New Jersey Nets. He became the youngest player to win three NBA championships at just 23 years old.
  • December 20, 2005 - Bryant scored 62 points in three quarters against the Dallas Mavericks. Entering the fourth quarter, Bryant outscored the entire Mavericks team 62–61, the only time a player has done this through three quarters since the introduction of the shot clock.
  • January 22, 2006 - Bryant scored a career-high 81 points in a 122–104 victory against the Toronto Raptors. Bryant's 81-point game was the second-highest point total in NBA history, surpassed only by Chamberlain's 100-point game in 1962.
  • March 3, 2006 - Kobe announces he will change his number from 8 to 24 for the start of the 2006-2007 season.
  • December 23, 2007 - Bryant became the youngest player (29 years, 122 days) to reach 20,000 points.
  • May 6, 2008 - Bryant was announced as the NBA's MVP for the 2007-2008 season.
  • June 14, 2009 - Kobe leads the Lakers to the NBA Championship for the 4th of his career and won the NBA Finals MVP award.
  • June 17, 2010 - Bryant and the Lakers win back to back NBA Championships with Kobe being named the Finals MVP. It was the fourth championship and second Finals MVP of his career.
  • February 20, 2011 - Kobe won his fourth All-Star Game MVP, tying Hall of Famer Bob Pettit for the most All-Star MVP awards.
  • December 5, 2012 - Bryant became the youngest player (34 years and 104 days) in league history to score 30,000 points, joining Hall of Famers Chamberlain, Jordan, Kareem Abdul-Jabbar, and Karl Malone as one of five players to reach that milestone.
  • December 14, 2014 - Bryant became the NBA's third all-time leading scorer, passing Michael Jordan (32,292).
  • November 29, 2015 - Bryant announced via The Players' Tribune that he will be retiring at the end of the season.
  • January 26, 2020 - dies at the age of 41.
